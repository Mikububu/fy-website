On Relationships and Tantra: The Energetic Debt You Carry

Bespoke Tantric experiences that provoke you to the core while transforming your life - by revealing the karmic debt you've been carrying and how to finally release it.

Jan 16, 2024

Since 2014, Forbidden-Yoga.com has designed personalized retreat scenarios for individuals and couples dealing with relationship challenges, burnout, low self-worth, depression, anxiety, social isolation, and sexual dysfunction. The organization curates customized experiences in global locations tailored to each participant's unique needs.

Clients enjoy flexibility in choosing retreat duration, location, and participants, enabling life-changing experiences focused on self-discovery and personal growth.

Michael Perin Wogenburg, founder and practitioner, clarifies that Forbidden Yoga doesn't distinguish between partnered and single individuals when offering Tantric rituals. The same ancient practices apply universally regardless of relationship status or sexual orientation.

The work emphasizes masculine and feminine as energetic principles rather than social categories. These principles operate at every biological level. "In a single-celled organism, masculine and feminine appear as anabolic and catabolic processes."

Wogenburg's observational method during rituals draws from Milton Erickson's hypnotherapy training-watching subtle bodily signals that reveal unconscious processes. During Tantric practice, energetic reality manifests through gesture, breath patterns, and presence quality.

Relationships with balanced masculine-feminine energy maintain dynamic equilibrium without chronic debt accumulation. When leaving such relationships, examining whether energetic debt remains is crucial. Unresolved debt travels forward into subsequent relationships and partnerships.

Karma represents this unpaid energetic obligation. Saṃskāra describes deep grooves worn into consciousness from repeated, unresolved experiences-like a record forcing the needle to replay the same patterns. Unresolved relationship dynamics create channels that intensify suffering with each repetition.

Kṣobh, from Tantric cosmology, means the primal disturbance breaking absolute stillness and creating duality. All manifestation emerges from this initial asymmetry. Every unresolved polarity perpetuates this original disturbance until balanced.

Psychology focuses on mind processes, but Tantric practice aims at manonāśa-mind destruction-since the mind itself creates duality and cannot resolve it.

The practice involves sitting, bringing awareness to the heart center, and allowing specific relationships to arise without mental analysis. The body reveals energetic truth through physical sensations and openness or contraction.

Bert Hellinger's family constellation work validated these principles. Over decades, he observed that unresolved family system imbalances transmit across generations. Excluded family members unconsciously repeat ancestral patterns-not through learned behavior but energetic structures.

During Forbidden Yoga rituals, similar constellations emerge. Participants unconsciously organize themselves according to hidden loyalties and unresolved debts, revealing through spatial positioning and somatic responses what the mind conceals.

Unresolved asymmetry doesn't remain isolated-it propagates through systems, creating compensatory distortions requiring resolution at the origin point.

Resolution requires returning energetically rather than physically. Through meditation and honest self-examination, feeling without protective narratives reveals what was actually true. The heart center (anāhata) finds this truth, as opposites coexist there without requiring mental resolution.

Only when energetic debt clears and masculine-feminine exchange flows without chronic asymmetry does genuine relationship emerge-not pattern repetition but authentic meeting between beings free from accumulated karma.
